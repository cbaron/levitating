    Brace Yourselves for the Coming ObamaCare Shock
    Dear Vinay,
    From the get-go — from before the law was even passed — we've been warning of the coming ObamaCare catastrophe.
    Writing today in the Wall Street Journal, Stanford University business and law professor Daniel Kessler notices that sounds of alarm are coming from some interesting and rather unexpected places:

    Montana Sen. Max Baucus, a Democrat, called it a "train wreck." A Democratic colleague, West Virginia's Sen. Jay Rockefeller, described the massive Affordable Care Act as "beyond comprehension." Henry Chao, the government's chief technical officer in charge of putting in place the insurance exchanges mandated by the law, was quoted in the Congressional Quarterly as saying "I'm pretty nervous . . . Let's just make sure it's not a third-world experience."

    These individuals are worried for good reason. The unpopular health-care law's rollout is going to be rough. It will also administer several price (and other) shocks to tens of millions of Americans.

    The litany of people who will be harmed by this law is as lengthy as it is disturbing.  Be sure to read all of Kessler's piece for some of the gory details, but his summation drives the point home:

    In total, it appears that there will be 30 million to 40 million people damaged in some fashion by the Affordable Care Act—more than one in 10 Americans. When that reality becomes clearer, the law is going to start losing its friends in the media, who are inclined to support the president and his initiatives. We'll hear about innocent victims who saw their premiums skyrocket, who were barred from seeing their usual doctor, who had their hours cut or lost their insurance entirely—all thanks to the faceless bureaucracy administering a federal law.

    We'll believe it about the media when we see it.  But Kessler is undeniably right about the coming shock to our health care system.
    There's something else that can help spur Congress to act to repeal, defund, and deauthorize this legislative nightmare: you and your activism.
    As we move into mid-2013, potential candidates for the House and Senate are beginning to make the first moves in running for office.  Ditto for incumbents seeking reelection.  We must know that they are fully committed to repealing ObamaCare before they can receive a single vote.
    That's why there's the ObamaCare Repeal Pledge.  You can go there now to see who in Congress has signed, and who has not.
    If your representatives haven't signed yet, click here to send them a message telling them that before they can earn your vote, you need to see their signature on the Pledge.
    In the end, it'll be your activism that will make the difference in saving America from the consequences of this awful law.
                                                                   Sincerely,
